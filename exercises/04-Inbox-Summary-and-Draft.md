# Exercise 4 — Inbox Summary to Coaching Readout & Draft

- **App:** Outlook on the web
- **Workflow:** Turn long Operations threads into briefings and action items, then draft the front-line readout, the unmapped-codes list, and a process-improvement note — in your voice
- **Material:** the four practice threads from the [Practice Inbox Pack](../assets/Practice-Inbox-Pack.md)
- **Estimated time:** 18 minutes
- **Difficulty:** Core

---

## Objective

Operations runs on email: coaching trends, vendor mapping questions, follow-up sweeps, process exceptions. This exercise uses Copilot in Outlook to **summarize** those threads, **extract action items**, and **draft** the replies and notes you'd otherwise write from scratch — always leaving the send button to you.

## Before you start

- [ ] You completed the pre-work and emailed yourself the four practice threads.
- [ ] Outlook on the web open; you can see the four threads by subject.

## What Copilot is doing here

Copilot in Outlook works over **the mail you can open**. It **summarizes** threads, pulls **action items, owners, and deadlines**, and **drafts** replies and notes. It **sends nothing** — every draft waits for you to review and click send.

---

## Part A — Catch up and brief

### Step 1 — Catch me up
```text
Catch me up on my Operations email from the last few days. Group by topic; for each give a one-line status, any deadline, and who owns the next step. Flag anything that needs a decision from me.
```

### Step 2 — Thread to a coaching readout
Open **Monthly Coaching Trends — Operations Readout** and run:
```text
Summarize this thread as a 5-bullet readout for the front-line leaders: the overall coaching trend, the Item Processing data-entry cluster, the Wire Services callback issue, the documentation-accuracy creep, and the High-severity callout. End with one line: "Coaching focus this month."
```

### Step 3 — Action items only
Open **90-Day Follow-Up Items — Q2 Review** and run:
```text
From this thread, list only the open action items as a checklist: owner, commitment, and due date. Leave out anything already resolved.
```

---

## Part B — Draft as a professional

### Step 4 — Reply in your voice
Open **Vendor File Mapping — Servicing Export to Template** and run:
```text
Draft a reply from me confirming I'll run the Q2 servicing export through the translation table, flag any codes that don't map (instead of guessing), and send back a short list of the unmapped rows — the row, the original code, and which field it's in — before anyone builds the upload file. Professional, three short paragraphs. Don't send — I'll review.
```

### Step 5 — Process-improvement note with placeholders
Open **Process Exception — Wire Services Callback Gap** and run:
```text
Draft a short process-improvement note for the Change Initiatives intake based on this thread: what's happening (the second-callback step is performed but not documented), the impact (looks like a skipped control in the record), and the proposed fix (add the step to the job aid, make the system field required, re-baseline the log). Leave the frequency as [COUNT] and the period as [PERIOD] for me to fill from the training-opportunity log. Factual tone. Don't send.
```

---

## Deliverable

A grouped inbox summary, a front-line coaching readout, a clean follow-up action-item checklist, a drafted vendor-mapping reply, and a process-improvement note with placeholders for the figures you'll verify.

## Verify before you rely on it

- [ ] The readout's claims match the threads — Copilot didn't invent a department or a number.
- [ ] The action-item list has a real owner and due date for each item; nothing already-resolved snuck in.
- [ ] The process-improvement note left `[COUNT]` and `[PERIOD]` as placeholders — fill them from the verified pivot in Exercise 3, don't trust a number Copilot guessed.
- [ ] Every draft is still **a draft** — nothing was sent.

## Key takeaways

- "Catch me up" + "action items only" clears an Operations inbox faster than reading top to bottom.
- **Draft with placeholders** for any figure that leaves the team — fill it from a verified source.
- Copilot drafts; **you own the voice and the send button**.
