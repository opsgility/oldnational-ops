# Pre-Work — Before the Copilot for Operations Session

**Do this the day before the session. It takes about 10–12 minutes.**

The session is hands-on across two apps. The Excel exercises use synthetic Old National Bank-style sample workbooks, and Copilot in Excel only works on files saved in your Microsoft 365 environment **with AutoSave on** — so the workbooks must be in **your OneDrive** before we start. The Outlook exercise works over a small **practice inbox** you build by emailing yourself a few sample threads.

---

## Why the setup matters

Copilot in Excel **requires** the file to be cloud-saved with AutoSave on — it will not run on a local-only workbook. Cloud-saved files also give you **version history** (Copilot edits cells directly and auto-saves; version history is your rollback). Copilot in Outlook works over **the mail you can open** — your own mailbox plus any mailbox you have delegate access to — so the practice threads you send yourself become the material Copilot summarizes and drafts replies to.

> **OneDrive, not SharePoint.** These exercises assume OneDrive only. You do **not** need SharePoint access for anything here. The upload template you build is the clean source — putting it into Docusign or a list afterward is a manual step you do yourself.

---

## Step 1 — Confirm you have access

If anything below is missing, contact your IT/Copilot administrator **before** the session.

- [ ] You can sign in to Microsoft 365 (office.com) with your ONB account.
- [ ] You have a **Microsoft 365 Copilot** license (open Excel → look for the **Copilot** button on the Home ribbon; open Outlook on the web → look for **Copilot** / **Summarize**).
- [ ] You can open **OneDrive** from office.com and see your files.
- [ ] You can open a workbook in **Excel for the web** and a message in **Outlook on the web** (our classroom baselines).

## Step 2 — Create a folder in OneDrive

1. Go to **office.com** and sign in.
2. Open **OneDrive**.
3. Click **+ Add new → Folder**.
4. Name it: **Copilot Operations Session**.

## Step 3 — Upload the sample workbooks

Upload **all** of the following into your **Copilot Operations Session** folder. Your instructor will provide these files (or a link).

## Step 4 — Build your practice inbox (for the Outlook exercise)

Open the [**Practice Inbox Pack**](../assets/Practice-Inbox-Pack.md). It contains four ready-made Operations email threads. For each one:

1. In Outlook, start a **new email to yourself** (your own address in the To line).
2. Copy the **subject line exactly** as written (e.g., `Monthly Coaching Trends — Operations Readout`) — you'll reference these names with `/` inside Copilot, so the exact name matters.
3. Paste the **first message** into the body and **send**.
4. When it arrives, **Reply to yourself** and paste the next message; repeat until the whole thread is in your inbox.

> **Faster option:** send just the first message of each thread, then grow them with a reply or two during the session. Copilot gives richer summaries when a thread has 3+ messages.

## Step 5 — Quick readiness check (2 minutes)

1. In OneDrive, open **Training-Opportunities.xlsx** in **Excel for the web**.
2. Confirm **AutoSave** is on (top-left toggle).
3. Click the **Copilot** button on the Home ribbon, and note the toggle above the prompt box: **Chat only** vs. **Allow editing** — we use both.
4. In Chat-only mode, type: `How many rows of data are on the Log sheet, and what are the column headers?`
5. Then open Outlook on the web, open the **Monthly Coaching Trends** thread, and click **Summarize**.
6. If both return a sensible answer, you're ready. If a Copilot button is missing or greyed out, contact your administrator before the session.

## Step 6 — One habit to bring

Before the session, find one **real formula** in a tracker or log you inherited and didn't write — a nested `IF`, a `VLOOKUP`, a date calculation. In Exercise 2 you'll learn to have Copilot explain formulas like it. Bring the curiosity, not the file.
